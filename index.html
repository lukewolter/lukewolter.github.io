Leadership Case Studies

EY — Building the Global Managed Services Solutions Portfolio (Multi-Billion-Dollar Revenue Stream)

Challenge → Rapidly growing Managed Services business lacked global consistency, client-ready assets, and standardized risk controls. Deal teams faced prolonged sales cycles, margin pressure, and high commercial/delivery risk due to fragmented contracting, pricing, and go-to-market approaches—especially as clients demanded AI-enabled and outcome-based services.

Strategy → Transition from regional silos to a centralized, insight-driven global portfolio management model. Prioritize reusable assets, embed risk reduction across the deal lifecycle, and accelerate innovation (AI solutions + flexible commercial models) to drive pipeline velocity, win rates, and margin expansion.

Execution → Over 8+ years as Global Solution Management Director, led a distributed global team and orchestrated cross-functional programs:
• Built product roadmaps and an industrial-scale asset factory (hundreds of high-quality playbooks, pursuit collateral, delivery methodologies).
• Created the firm-wide Global Managed Services Legal Toolkit (contract templates, negotiation guidance, pre-approved positions on top 30 commercial terms).
• Delivered technology costing/pricing guidance, channel conflict policies, and a top-100 deal risk analysis that triggered mandatory contractual changes.
• Enabled service lines with Win Centers, Discovery sites, and wavespace kits.

Results → Transformed Managed Services into one of EY’s fastest-growing and most profitable solution areas | Established a “single source of truth” asset ecosystem now used globally | Eliminated most high-risk ad-hoc negotiations | Reduced commercial risk exposure by hundreds of millions annually | Shortened sales cycles and improved win rates | Positioned EY as a market leader in AI-enhanced, outcome-based managed services.

Leadership Lesson → “Scale doesn’t come from heroics in silos—it comes from building reusable infrastructure that turns individual wins into organizational muscle memory. Invest disproportionately in the ‘invisible’ plumbing (legal toolkits, pricing guidance, risk frameworks); when done right, it becomes the unfair advantage competitors can’t replicate overnight.”
